Your Guide to Online OCR: Extracting Text from Images

What is OCR and How Does It Work?

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) is a technology that converts different types of documents, such as scanned paper documents, PDF files, or images captured by a digital camera, into editable and searchable data. Instead of manually retyping text from an image, our OCR tool automatically recognizes the characters and extracts them for you in seconds, saving you valuable time and effort.

Common Uses for Image to Text Conversion

This powerful tool can simplify a wide range of tasks. Here are a few examples:

  • Digitizing Documents: Convert printed contracts, invoices, or receipts into digital text for easy storage and searching.
  • Studying & Note-Taking: Quickly copy text from a textbook, presentation slide, or a friend's notes without typing.
  • Data Entry: Extract information from business cards, posters, or screenshots to paste into spreadsheets or contact lists.

Tips for a More Accurate Text Extraction

1. Use High-Quality Images:

The clearer your image, the better the result. Ensure the text is in focus, well-lit, and not blurry. A higher resolution generally leads to higher accuracy.

2. Ensure Good Contrast:

Text that clearly stands out from the background (e.g., black text on a white background) is much easier for the OCR engine to recognize.

3. Check the Language Setting:

Make sure you have selected the correct language from the dropdown menu before starting the extraction process. This is crucial for accurate recognition of special characters and accents.
